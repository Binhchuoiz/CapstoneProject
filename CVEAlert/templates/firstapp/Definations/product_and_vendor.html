About Product

Definition: Product is the software or hardware product that is affected by the vulnerability.

Examples: Examples of products include operating systems, web browsers, application software, network hardware, etc.

Notation: Products are denoted by their full name or standard abbreviation. For example: Windows 10, Apache Struts 2, Cisco IOS, etc.

About Vendor

Definition: Vendor is the supplier or developer of the product that is affected by the vulnerability.

Examples: Examples of vendors include Microsoft, Oracle, Cisco, etc.

Notation: Vendors are denoted by their full name.

Conclusion

Both product and vendor play important roles in determining the CVE ID:

The CVE ID is generated based on information about the product and vendor.

Using the product and vendor helps users easily search and identify vulnerabilities that affect them.
